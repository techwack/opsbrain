# Near-Miss Report NM-2023-058
Date: 2023-11-01 | Unit: 2 | Equipment: Pump P-204 | Severity: High potential

## Description
During normal operation, the field operator on round observed a small pool of crude
(~0.5 L) beneath the P-204 pump skid, originating from the mechanical seal gland. A steam
tracing line and a hot pump casing (surface ~120 °C) were within 1 m of the leak,
creating an auto-ignition potential. No fire occurred. Area was barricaded and the pump
was switched to standby P-205.

## Immediate Cause
Mechanical seal (John Crane Type 2100) leak — the second seal leak on P-204 within five
weeks (see WO-2023-0991).

## Root Cause
Inadequate seal flush margin at elevated suction temperature; flush plan (API Plan 11
alone) insufficient for the service condition. Underlying systemic cause: no RCFA was
triggered after the first repeat failure.

## Regulatory Note
Per OISD-STD-105 Clause 5.1, two seal failures within 12 months on the same pump should
have triggered a documented RCFA. Per Clause 7.1, the hot-surface exposure qualifies as a
reportable high-potential near-miss.

## Corrective Actions
1. Upgrade flush to API Plan 11 + 62 (quench). [Done — WO-2023-0991]
2. Conduct formal RCFA on P-204 seal failures. [Action owner: Reliability Engg.]
3. Add P-204 to the critical-equipment vibration & seal-temp watchlist.
