# OISD-STD-105 (Excerpt) — Work Permit System & Rotating Equipment Safety
Oil Industry Safety Directorate, Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas (paraphrased for demo)

## Clause 4.2 — Permit to Work (PTW)
A valid written Permit-to-Work shall be issued before any maintenance on hydrocarbon
service equipment. The permit must record isolation points, LOTO confirmation, and gas
test results where applicable. Hot work within 15 m of a hydrocarbon source requires a
separate hot-work permit and continuous gas monitoring.

## Clause 5.1 — Mechanical Seal Integrity (pumps in hydrocarbon service)
Pumps handling Class A/B petroleum products shall be fitted with a seal flush/quench
arrangement appropriate to the service temperature. Single seals on light hydrocarbon
service above 60 °C shall include a quench (e.g., API Plan 62) or be upgraded to a dual
seal. Repeat seal failures (more than two in any 12-month period on the same pump) shall
trigger a documented Root Cause Failure Analysis (RCFA).

## Clause 5.4 — Vibration Monitoring
Critical rotating equipment shall be monitored against ISO 10816 limits. Trending data
shall be reviewed monthly; any sustained upward trend shall generate a predictive work
order before the alarm threshold is reached.

## Clause 6.3 — Isolation Integrity
Positive isolation must be verifiable before LOTO. A valve that passes (fails to fully
isolate) shall not be relied upon as a sole isolation point; double block and bleed or a
spade/blind shall be used.

## Clause 7.1 — Near-Miss Reporting
All near-misses, including minor leaks and hot-surface exposure events, shall be reported
within 24 hours and reviewed in the monthly safety committee. Recurrence of a near-miss
category requires corrective action tracked to closure.
